Leftover Rice & Salmon Bowl

This post may include affiliate links. Thank you for your support.

Discover a delightful Salmon Rice Bowl, a delicious and convenient way to breathe new life into leftover salmon and rice! This easy-to-make dish boasts a zesty sauce blend of soy sauce, mayonnaise, sesame oil, and a touch of sriracha, complementing flaked salmon and rice. Topped with creamy avocado and sesame seeds, it's a quick, flavorsome meal that redefines leftovers with every bite!

Ingredients

  • 170 grams leftover cooked salmon filets

  • 380 grams leftover cooked rice

  • 1 ice cube

  • Â½ avocado, sliced

  • 30 milliliters soy sauce

  • 15 milliliters mayonnaise

  • 5 milliliters sesame oil

  • 10 milliliters sriracha

  • 5 grams sesame seeds (optional)

Instructions

  1. Flake the leftover cooked salmon into smaller pieces. Reheat both the salmon and the rice in the microwave. Ice Cube Tip: If the rice has dried out slightly, place a single ice cube over it in a covered microwave-safe bowl. Reheat it in the microwave for about a minute to restore moisture.

  2. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, mayonnaise, sesame oil, and sriracha

  3. In a large bowl, mix the prepared sauce with the reheated rice and salmon until evenly coated.

  4. Place the sliced avocado on top of the rice and salmon mixture. Sprinkle with sesame seeds for an added touch.

  5. Enjoy your flavorful and convenient salmon rice bowl!
